# 👥 HR Analytics — Employee Attrition Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes HR data to identify the key factors driving employee attrition. By examining patterns across departments, job roles, salary bands, tenure, age, and gender, the project delivers actionable insights and recommendations to help management reduce turnover and improve employee retention.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem
High employee attrition is costly — it affects productivity, morale, and recruitment budgets. This project answers critical HR questions such as:
- Which departments and job roles have the highest attrition rates?
- Does salary level significantly impact an employee's likelihood of leaving?
- Are certain age groups or tenure ranges more at risk?
- What targeted interventions can reduce attrition most effectively?

## 🔄 Project Workflow
1. **Data Collection** — Loaded HR department dataset containing employee demographics, roles, salaries, and attrition status
2. **Data Cleaning** — Cleaned and prepared data using Power Query (handled nulls, standardized categories, corrected data types)
3. **KPI Development** — Designed attrition rate KPIs and workforce metrics for clear performance tracking
4. **Multi-dimensional Analysis** — Analyzed attrition by:
   - Department
   - Job Role
   - Salary Band
   - Education Level
   - Age Group
   - Gender
   - Tenure
5. **Dashboard Creation** — Built interactive Power BI dashboards to visualize attrition trends and workforce insights
6. **Recommendations** — Delivered targeted HR intervention recommendations based on findings

---

## 📊 Key Findings
- Identified highest attrition concentrated in specific job roles and lower salary bands
- Certain departments showed disproportionately high turnover relative to their size
- Employees in early tenure stages (under 2 years) showed significantly higher attrition risk
- Age group analysis revealed younger employees had higher attrition tendencies
- Recommendations focused on targeted retention strategies for high-risk segments
